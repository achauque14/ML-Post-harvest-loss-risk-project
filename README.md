## Overview

This repository contains the complete LaTeX source for the above B.Sc. dissertation. The project develops and evaluates machine learning models capable of predicting post-harvest loss risk for smallholder agro-businesses in developing countries, using structured survey data from Nigeria.

Post-harvest losses — the reduction in quantity or quality of agricultural produce between harvest and consumption — affect up to 30–40% of output in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. This work demonstrates that simple, interpretable ML models (Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest) trained on low-cost tabular data can serve as the backbone of practical decision support tools for smallholder farmers.

---

## Key Findings

- Approximately **36.4%** of observed smallholder households experienced a post-harvest loss event.
- **Storage method** and **storage duration** are the strongest predictors of loss risk.
- **Random Forest** achieved the highest AUC-ROC (0.9938), followed closely by Decision Tree (0.9920).
- Loss rates are significantly higher in **rural** and **northern** regions of Nigeria, highlighting infrastructure gaps.
- Even simple ML models can deliver high predictive accuracy on small, structured datasets — making them viable for resource-constrained settings.
